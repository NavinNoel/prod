Whitehall Mennonite Church belongs to the Franconia Mennonite Conference which is one of 21 area conferences in the United States that forms Mennonite Church USA. Please use the links below if you like to find out more.

Ripple

God is making a difference in peoples' lives, and we invite you to join our community of Rippl-ers. Each person is on a journey called Life, and at Ripple we would like to hear your story.

 Franconia Mennonite Conference

Franconia Mennonite Conference is one of 21 conferences that comprise Mennonite Church USA, is a network of congregations, ministries, partnerships and initiatives continuing to emerge out of over 300 years of Anabaptist witness and faith in the Western Hemisphere that began in Philadelphia.
Peace and Justice Committee

PJC is a joint conference committee, with support from both Franconia Mennonite Conference and Eastern District Conference. We can help you identify resources, speakers, or training opportunities for your work in many peace-related topics

 Mennonite Church USA

Mennonites are part of the larger Christian expression called Anabaptism (meaning "baptized again"). Our identity is founded in 475 years of witness since the Reformation. We enjoy a spiritual kinship with over a million Anabaptist related Christians worldwide.
Mennonite Central Committee

MCC seeks to demonstrate God's love by working among people suffering from poverty, conflict, oppression and natural disaster. MCC sends people, food and material goods to communities recovering from war and natural disasters.

 Mennonite Disaster Service

Mennonite Disaster Service is a volunteer network through which various constituencies of the Anabaptist church can respond to those affected by disasters in Canada and the United States.
Mennonite Publishing Network

The publishing ministry of Mennonite Church USA and Mennonite Church Canada.

 Spruce Lake Retreat

Located in the Pocono Mountains of Pennsylvania (PA), Spruce Lake offers a Christian retreat and conference center, summer children's and youth camp, Bible-based educational ministries and a family campground.
